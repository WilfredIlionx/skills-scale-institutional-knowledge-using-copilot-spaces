# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
Scrum Masters facilitate agile practices and ceremonies, ensuring the team follows scrum principles effectively. They serve the team by removing impediments, coaching on agile methodologies, and fostering a culture of continuous improvement.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate scrum ceremonies (daily standups, sprint planning, retrospectives, sprint reviews)
- Remove blockers and impediments that hinder team progress
- Coach the team on agile practices and self-organization
- Shield the team from external distractions and interruptions
- Foster collaboration and continuous improvement within the team
- Track and communicate team velocity and sprint metrics

### Goals
- Enable the team to deliver maximum value each sprint
- Create a culture of transparency, inspection, and adaptation
- Continuously improve team processes and efficiency
- Ensure team adherence to agile principles and practices

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint ceremonies facilitation
- One-on-ones with team members to address impediments
- Collaboration with Project Managers on resource and timeline alignment
- Regular touchpoints with Product Managers for backlog refinement

### Interactions
- Works closely with **Project Managers** to align on delivery schedules and risk management
- Supports **Developers** by removing blockers and facilitating technical discussions
- Collaborates with **Product Manager** to ensure backlog clarity and sprint goals alignment
- Partners with other Scrum Masters for organizational process improvements

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers plan, coordinate, and oversee software releases to ensure smooth deployment to production environments. They ensure compliance with release processes, manage release schedules, and communicate with stakeholders about release status.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and coordinate release schedules across multiple teams
- Ensure compliance with release processes and quality gates
- Manage release documentation and release notes
- Coordinate deployment activities and rollback procedures
- Communicate release schedules and status to stakeholders
- Track and report on release metrics and post-release issues
- Maintain release calendar and coordinate with dependent teams

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, on-time releases with minimal disruption
- Reduce deployment risks through standardized processes
- Ensure stakeholder awareness and alignment on release timelines
- Continuously improve release velocity and quality

### Typical Communication
- Weekly release planning meetings with engineering teams
- Release status updates and go/no-go decisions
- Post-release reports and metrics to stakeholders
- Coordination with operations and infrastructure teams

### Interactions
- Collaborates with **Developers** and QA teams to verify release readiness
- Aligns with **Product Manager** on release priorities and feature readiness
- Coordinates with **Project Manager** on timeline dependencies and risks
- Communicates with **Stakeholder Representatives** on release schedules and impacts

---

## Documentation Lead

### Role Summary
Documentation Leads own the strategy, quality, and accessibility of technical and process documentation. They ensure that documentation is accurate, up-to-date, and serves the needs of both internal teams and external users.

### Responsibilities
- Define documentation standards and best practices
- Oversee creation and maintenance of technical and process documentation
- Ensure documentation accuracy and consistency across projects
- Manage documentation tools and publishing workflows
- Coordinate with subject matter experts to capture knowledge
- Review and approve documentation changes
- Track documentation coverage and identify gaps

### Goals
- Maintain comprehensive, accessible documentation for all audiences
- Enable team members to find information quickly and easily
- Reduce knowledge silos and support effective onboarding
- Ensure documentation reflects current processes and systems

### Typical Communication
- Documentation review sessions with contributors
- Coordination meetings with Project Managers and technical leads
- Status updates on documentation initiatives and backlogs
- Training sessions on documentation tools and standards

### Interactions
- Coordinates with **Project Managers** to document processes and project artifacts
- Works with **Developers** to create and maintain technical documentation
- Collaborates with **Stakeholder Representatives** to ensure documentation meets user needs
- Partners with **UX Designer** to improve documentation usability and accessibility

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers advocate for end-users and design intuitive, accessible experiences. They conduct user research, create design specifications, and ensure that products meet usability standards and user expectations.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications
- Define and maintain design systems and style guides
- Ensure accessibility and usability standards are met
- Validate designs through user feedback and testing
- Collaborate on information architecture and user flows
- Advocate for user needs in product decisions

### Goals
- Create intuitive, user-centered product experiences
- Ensure consistency in design across all product touchpoints
- Validate design decisions with data and user feedback
- Improve key usability metrics and user satisfaction

### Typical Communication
- Design review sessions with Product Managers and Developers
- User research findings and usability test reports
- Design specs and prototypes shared via collaboration tools
- Feedback sessions with stakeholders and end-users

### Interactions
- Consults with **Product Manager** to understand user needs and business goals
- Collaborates with **Developers** to ensure design feasibility and implementation quality
- Receives feedback from **Stakeholder Representatives** on design direction
- Works with **Documentation Lead** to ensure user-facing documentation is clear and usable

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge the gap between business needs and technical solutions. They analyze business processes, translate requirements, and ensure that development efforts align with business objectives and deliver measurable value.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and analyze business requirements from stakeholders
- Translate business needs into clear, actionable technical requirements
- Conduct process analysis and identify improvement opportunities
- Create business cases and ROI analyses for initiatives
- Facilitate requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Validate that solutions meet business needs and acceptance criteria
- Support change management and user adoption efforts

### Goals
- Ensure technical solutions align with business objectives
- Improve business processes through data-driven insights
- Reduce ambiguity in requirements and acceptance criteria
- Maximize return on investment for development efforts

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Business requirement documents and user stories
- Process flow diagrams and analysis reports
- Status updates on requirements clarification and validation

### Interactions
- Works closely with **Product Manager** to refine product vision and requirements
- Supports **Project Manager** in requirements clarity and scope definition
- Collaborates with **Stakeholder Representatives** to understand business needs
- Provides requirements context and clarification to **Developers**

---

## Stakeholder Representatives

### Role Summary
Stakeholder Representatives bring the perspective of user groups, business units, or customers into the development process. They review deliverables, provide feedback, and ensure that solutions meet the needs of their constituents.

### Responsibilities
- Represent the interests and needs of specific user or business groups
- Review and approve deliverables, designs, and requirements
- Provide feedback throughout the project lifecycle
- Validate that solutions meet business and user expectations
- Communicate project updates and impacts to their constituents
- Participate in acceptance testing and user validation
- Escalate concerns and risks to appropriate project leadership

### Goals
- Ensure solutions meet the needs of their represented groups
- Provide timely, actionable feedback to the team
- Maintain visibility into project progress and decisions
- Support successful adoption and change management

### Typical Communication
- Regular status updates and demo sessions
- Feedback on designs, requirements, and deliverables
- Approval gates for key milestones
- Updates to constituents on project progress and impacts

### Interactions
- Interface with **Project Manager** for status updates and milestone reviews
- Collaborate with **Product Manager** to validate product direction and priorities
- Provide feedback to **UX Designer** on design direction and usability
- Work with **Business Analyst** to clarify business needs and requirements
- Participate in demos and reviews with **Developers**
